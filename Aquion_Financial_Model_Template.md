## BREAK-EVEN ANALYSIS

- Contribution margin: ₱20,000

### Break-Even Units (Annual):
₱1,416,000 ÷ ₱20,000 = **71 units per year**

## SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

### What if sales are 50% lower? (25 units in Year 1)

**Revenue: ₱2,750,000**  
**COGS: ₱2,287,500**  
**Gross Profit: ₱462,500**  
**Operating Expenses: ₱700,000** (reduced due to lower activity)  
**Net Income: -₱237,500** (LOSS)

**Action:** Need to reduce fixed costs or secure more funding.

### What if material costs increase 20%?

**Impact on gross margin:**
- Basic: 14.2% → 6.8% (ouch!)
- Refresh: 18.8% → 10.4%
- Premium: 21.6% → 12.9%

**Action:** Must raise prices or negotiate better supplier terms.

### What if you get 3 months delayed in sales?

**Cash flow impact:**
- 3 extra months of expenses: ₱150,000-300,000
- Delayed revenue pushes break-even to Month 9-12

**Action:** Have 6-month cash runway, not just 3.

## KEY FINANCIAL METRICS (TRACK MONTHLY)

### Efficiency Metrics:
- **COGS as % of Revenue:** Target <70%
- **Gross Margin:** Target >25% by Year 2
- **Operating Expense Ratio:** Target <30%
- **Net Profit Margin:** Target 5% Year 1, 15% Year 2, 25% Year 3

### Cash Metrics:
- **Cash Balance:** Never below ₱100,000
- **Days Cash on Hand:** Target 60+ days
- **Accounts Receivable Days:** Target <30 days (with deposit model)
- **Inventory Turns:** Target 6x per year (every 2 months)

### Sales Metrics:
- **Customer Acquisition Cost:** Target <₱10,000 per customer
- **Lifetime Value:** Target ₱150,000+ (initial sale + 3 years maintenance)
- **CAC:LTV Ratio:** Target 1:15 or better
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue (from maintenance):** Target ₱50,000+ by Month 12

## FORMULA CHEAT SHEET (For Excel)

### Gross Margin:
```
= (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue * 100
```

### Break-Even Units:
```
= Fixed Costs / (Selling Price - Variable Cost per Unit)
```

### Cash Runway (months):
```
= Current Cash Balance / Monthly Burn Rate
```

### Customer Lifetime Value:
```
= (Average Order Value x Number of Purchases) + (Maintenance Revenue x Years)
```

### Customer Acquisition Cost:
```
= Total Sales & Marketing Expenses / Number of New Customers
```
